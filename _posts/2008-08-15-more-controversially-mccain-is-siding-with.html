More controversially, McCain is siding with telecommunications companies over the issue of “net neutrality.” His policy, shaped by telco-friendly ex-Federal Communications Commission Michael Powell, would oppose laws that seek to regulate which users can use how much Internet bandwidth at what price. Obama has come down on the other side of the net neutrality issue, siding with those who believe the telcos’ ability to regulate Internet availability is monopolistic and hinders innovation.
