Dec.23 and Dec. 30, 2013  edition of the New Yorker: Rebecca Curtis: “The Christmas Miracle”. A woman visits her sister and family over christmas in a story that paints a brutal but real picture of a family with its fair share of troubles. Coyotes are eating the cats, the pedophile uncle preys on the nieces and the protagonist suffers from halluncinations that brings out the truth that no one wants to hear. Curtis is also the author of “Twenty Grand," a collection of short stories.
